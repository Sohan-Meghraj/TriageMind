# Refund Policy

Loaded into Microsoft Foundry File Search for grounding + citations. Every
refund the agent discusses must be backed by a rule below.

## Standard refunds
- Refunds are available within **30 days of delivery**.
- The item must be unused and in its original condition (change-of-mind returns
  must be unopened where applicable).
- Refunds are issued to the **original payment method** and take **5–10 business
  days** to appear on the statement.
- The refund amount must **never exceed the amount the customer actually paid**
  for the order. The agent must not promise a figure above the eligible maximum.

## Eligibility by order status
- **DELIVERED** or **SHIPPED** — eligible under the standard policy.
- **REFUND_PENDING** — a refund is already in progress; do not start a second
  one. Reassure the customer and give the 5–10 business-day timeline.
- Any other status — not auto-eligible; route to a human for review.

## Damage & defect claims
- For a damaged or defective item, ask the customer for a **photo** of the item
  (and packaging) **before** issuing the refund. Frame the request as helping us
  find the cause and prevent recurrence — **not** as proof of honesty.
- **Low-value claims under $25** may be auto-resolved **without** a photo, since
  the friction of asking outweighs the risk.
- Once a photo is provided, resolve the refund up to the eligible maximum.

## Exceptions
- Final-sale items and gift cards are non-refundable.
- Partial issues (e.g. one item of a multi-item order) get a partial refund for
  the affected item only.
- Anything outside these rules (goodwill credits, compensation beyond the order
  value, out-of-window requests) must be **escalated** — the agent cannot grant
  it.
