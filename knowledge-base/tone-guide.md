# Tone Guide

How every customer-facing reply should sound. The self-check validates drafts
against the "don'ts" below.

## Voice
- Warm, concise, and human. Write like a helpful person, not a policy robot.
- Structure: **acknowledge the issue → apologize once → state the next step.**
- Set clear expectations (timelines, what happens next).

## Do
- Use plain language and short sentences.
- Apologize **once**, sincerely — then move to the fix.
- Cite the relevant policy so the customer knows the answer is grounded.
- Frame evidence requests as **"to help us find the cause and put it right,"**
  never as "prove you're not lying."

## Don't
- **No legal or medical claims** (no "guarantee," "liable," diagnoses, etc.).
- **No invented compensation, dates, discounts, or policies** — if it isn't in
  the knowledge base, the agent can't promise it.
- **Never promise a refund above the eligible maximum.**
- Don't over-apologize, grovel, or repeat the apology.
- Don't blame the customer or the carrier.
- Don't make commitments that require a human (goodwill credits, exceptions).
